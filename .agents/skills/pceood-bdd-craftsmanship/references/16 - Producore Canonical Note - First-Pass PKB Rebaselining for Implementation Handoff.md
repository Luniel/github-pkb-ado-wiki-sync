
## Producore Canonical Note — First-Pass PKB Rebaselining for Implementation Handoff

### Status

Canonical applied note.

This note defines an applied method for rebaselining a valid but still immature first-pass Product Knowledge Base (PKB) into a safe implementation-driving subset for downstream Engineering and AI-assisted coding work.

It derives from:

- Producore Canonical Model Definition
- Producore Canonical Guide - PKBDD Operating Doctrine
- Producore Canonical Note - First-Pass PKB Bootstrapping
- Producore Canonical Guide - Producore Engineering Doctrine
- Producore Canonical Guide - ADO Repo Wiki PKB Production Standard

It does not define new ontology, new authority rules, new lifecycle mechanics, new structural ownership rules, new PKBDD craftsmanship rules, new ADO host-platform rules, or new engineering doctrine.

---

### 1. Purpose

This note defines how to convert a valid but still immature first-pass PKB into a narrowed, doctrine-safe implementation-driving subset without losing preserved continuity or promoting unresolved meaning into hidden authority.

Its purpose is to prevent a recurring structural error:

- treating a valid first-pass PKB as though every page within it is equally implementation-driving merely because the PKB itself is valid

This method applies in:

- AI-assisted first implementation of a product or solution from immature source material
- first implementation handoff after retrospective PKB reconstruction
- recovery from historically fragmented product knowledge
- rebaselining of broad strategic notes into a bounded first implementation slice
- narrowing a benchmark first-pass PKB before handing it to Engineering or a coding AI

---

### 2. Scope Boundary

This note does not define:

- what a Capability is
- what Behavior is
- what makes a specification binding
- new lifecycle states or transitions
- PKB page-type doctrine
- ADO Repo Wiki topology, naming, or navigation mechanics
- RMF operating law
- engineering realization practice itself

Those concerns remain governed by higher canonical sources.

This note defines only how the existing canonical distinctions are applied when a valid first-pass PKB must be narrowed into a safe implementation-driving subset without structural dishonesty.

---

### 3. Core Distinction

A valid first-pass PKB is not the same thing as an implementation-driving PKB subset.

A first-pass PKB may validly preserve:

- broad continuity
- current-state capture
- governed approximations
- unresolved meaning
- provisional capability framing
- open strategic direction
- traceability and rationale continuity

Those surfaces are often necessary.

They are not all equally safe as direct build authority.

Therefore:

- first-pass validity must not be confused with implementation readiness
- preserved continuity must not be confused with build-ready contract scope
- unresolved meaning must not be pushed silently downstream into code, tests, or work-item wording
- narrowing is a governed applied step, not an excuse to throw away preserved continuity

---

### 4. Rebaselining Objective

The objective of rebaselining is to establish, from within a valid first-pass PKB, the bounded subset that is actually safe to drive implementation.

That requires the rebaselined subset to do five things at once:

1. preserve continuity with the broader first-pass PKB
2. identify what is actually implementation-driving now
3. identify what remains context, continuity, approximation, current-state capture, or unresolved meaning
4. prevent downstream invention of missing behavioral authority
5. keep future-direction and deferred scope visible without allowing them to masquerade as current build authority

Rebaselining therefore aims at safe narrowing, not at pretending the broader PKB is already uniformly mature.

---

### 5. When Rebaselining Is Required

Rebaselining is required when any of the following are true:

- the first-pass PKB preserves materially unresolved policy or evaluative logic
- the first-pass PKB contains broad architecture ambition beyond what is safe to build now
- current-state capture and target-state direction coexist in the same solution area
- capability confidence or boundary stability is materially uneven
- Engineering or coding AI would otherwise be forced to infer missing behavior
- support-selection or scope-selection decisions materially constrain what can be built now
- the PKB contains more preserved continuity than mature contract authority

If none of those are true, the rebaselining need may be low or absent.

If several are true, rebaselining is mandatory before implementation handoff.

---

### 6. Inputs to Rebaselining

Rebaselining takes as input a valid first-pass PKB and may also use:

- preserved source reconciliation
- current-state capture
- governed approximations
- open questions and unresolved-meaning surfaces
- support-selection surfaces
- capability inventory or capability-candidate surfaces
- decision and rationale surfaces
- traceability notes
- implementation constraints that are already explicitly preserved in governed form

These inputs may inform narrowing.

They do not become implementation-driving merely by being reviewed during narrowing.

---

### 7. Rebaselining Outcome

A successful rebaseline produces a visibly bounded implementation-driving subset that distinguishes among:

- implementation-driving capability surfaces
- implementation-driving behavioral contract surfaces
- contextual but non-driving continuity surfaces
- current-state reference surfaces
- governed approximations retained for continuity
- unresolved meaning that still blocks or constrains downstream work
- deferred or excluded support not to be implemented now

The outcome must make it materially clear what is safe to hand downstream and what is present only as continuity or context.

---

### 8. Rebaselining Sequence

#### Step 1 - Confirm First-Pass Validity Before Narrowing

Do not begin by narrowing a PKB that is not yet valid as a first-pass PKB.

If the PKB still fails basic first-pass validity because it preserves too little continuity or misrepresents authority, correct that first.

Rebaselining is not a substitute for valid first-pass bootstrapping.

#### Step 2 - Identify the Candidate Implementation Slice

Identify the bounded implementation slice being considered.

This slice must be stated in terms of:

- what support is actually intended now
- what candidate capabilities appear needed now
- what supporting continuity must remain visible to interpret that slice honestly

Do not define the slice merely as “whatever seems easiest to code first.”

#### Step 3 - Separate Preserved Continuity from Build Authority

For each relevant artifact or artifact family in the first-pass PKB, determine whether it is presently functioning as:

- implementation-driving authority
- continuity-preserving context
- current-state reference
- governed approximation
- unresolved blocker or constraint
- deferred or excluded support

Do not treat these as the same thing.

#### Step 4 - Confidence-Band the Relevant Capability Surfaces

For each capability or capability candidate relevant to the implementation slice, assess whether it is:

- high-confidence and implementation-driving now
- real but still boundary-unstable
- preserved for continuity but not safe to build directly
- too provisional to use as implementation-driving structure now

This is not a new ontology.

It is an applied narrowing judgment.

#### Step 5 - Determine the Strongest Safe Artifact Family Per Area

For each relevant behavior area, determine the strongest safe artifact family for handoff:

- mature enough draft behavioral specification
- current-state behavior capture only
- governed approximation only
- support page only
- unresolved blocker requiring clarification before handoff
- no downstream handoff yet

Do not force every relevant area into a draft behavioral specification merely to make the handoff corpus look cleaner.

#### Step 6 - Mark the Non-Handoff Surfaces Explicitly

Anything relevant but not safe as implementation-driving authority must remain visible as one of:

- context
- continuity
- current-state reference
- governed approximation
- unresolved blocker
- deferred support
- excluded support

Do not delete such material merely because it is not part of the build subset.

#### Step 7 - Assemble the Narrowed Handoff Package

Create a visibly bounded implementation-driving package that contains:

- only the capabilities actually being used as build structure now
- only the specifications or equivalent governed surfaces safe for implementation now
- the support surfaces necessary to interpret those safely
- explicit blockers and exclusions where they materially constrain the build

This package may be physically represented as:
- a narrowed subtree
- a clearly governed handoff view
- a build-slice index or hub surface
- a controlled package of linked pages

This note does not dictate host-platform materialization mechanics.

#### Step 8 - Validate the Handoff Boundary

Before handoff, test whether Engineering or coding AI could still be forced to invent:

- missing behavior
- missing scope boundaries
- missing distinction between current-state and target-state
- missing distinction between context and contract
- missing treatment of unresolved evaluative logic

If yes, the rebaseline is not yet sufficient.

---

### 9. Benchmark Minimum Artifact Families for Rebaselined Handoff

A benchmark rebaselined handoff package should normally make visible, where relevant to the build slice:

- handoff entry or orientation surface
- implementation-driving capability surfaces
- implementation-driving behavioral contract surfaces
- explicit current-state reference surfaces where current-state knowledge materially constrains the work
- explicit governed approximations where approximation is preserved but not promoted
- explicit unresolved blockers where clarification is still required before implementation of a given area
- explicit deferred or excluded support surfaces where those materially affect interpretation
- rationale or decision continuity where the build slice would otherwise be misread
- traceability continuity where reverse-engineered or implementation-informed meaning materially affects safe interpretation

This does not require that all these surfaces exist in every case.

It does require that they not be silently collapsed.

---

### 10. Benchmark Root Package Pattern for Rebaselined Handoff

A benchmark rebaselined handoff should normally provide a visible entry surface that:

- identifies the intended implementation slice
- states what is in scope now
- states what remains out of scope, deferred, or blocked
- points to the capabilities and behavioral surfaces actually safe to build
- points separately to continuity/context surfaces still needed for interpretation
- preserves visibility of unresolved meaning that still constrains the work

This handoff entry surface is not a substitute for the underlying PKB.

It is a governed narrowing surface.

---

### 11. Benchmark Capability-Subtree Pattern for Rebaselined Handoff

For each capability included in the implementation slice, a benchmark rebaselined subtree should make it materially obvious which child surfaces are:

- implementation-driving behavioral specifications
- current-state reference only
- governed approximations
- open questions or unresolved blockers
- rationale or decision support
- traceability support
- additional aspects such as visual design where relevant

A reader must not have to guess which of those are build-driving and which are continuity-preserving only.

This does not require one naming convention.

It requires visible role separation.

---

### 12. Artifact-Family Decision Rules During Rebaselining

#### 12.1 Create or Retain a Capability Page When

Retain or create a capability page when:

- the capability boundary is strong enough to anchor downstream work now
- the capability is part of the actual implementation slice
- the capability page helps preserve capability identity and responsibility boundary so implementation structure does not masquerade as capability identity

Do not rely on an implementation module, screen, work item, or architecture note as the surrogate capability anchor.

#### 12.2 Use a Support Page When

Use or retain a support page when:

- the relevant knowledge is orienting, selecting, deferring, excluding, or rationalizing rather than expressing behavior contract
- the area still preserves solution continuity more than contract readiness
- the information materially affects interpretation but would corrupt a specification page if forced into it

#### 12.3 Use Current-State Capture When

Use or retain current-state capture when:

- stable current behavior must be preserved to avoid misunderstanding, regression, or false target-state assumption
- the implementation slice must interact with current reality before fuller target-state clarification is complete
- current-state understanding is real and important but not yet target-state authority

#### 12.4 Use a Governed Approximation When

Use or retain a governed approximation when:

- a directional rule or intended behavior is visible and important
- target-state precision is still insufficient for clean specification
- preserving the approximation explicitly is safer than leaving it implicit
- the approximation is not being silently promoted into hidden authority

#### 12.5 Use a Draft Behavioral Specification When

Use or retain a draft target-state behavioral specification only when all of the following are true:

- the behavior belongs clearly to a capability included in the implementation slice
- the event boundary is sufficiently knowable
- the start-state and resulting-state logic can be expressed without local invention
- the remaining unresolvedness does not destroy the usefulness of the draft as an implementation-driving contract surface
- the specification is actually safer than a weaker artifact family for this area

#### 12.6 Treat an Area as Blocked When

Treat an area as blocked from downstream handoff when:

- unresolved meaning materially affects behavior interpretation
- scoring or evaluative logic remains too immature to govern safely
- architecture ambition is still being mistaken for build authority
- capability boundaries remain too unstable to anchor implementation
- current-state and target-state remain too collapsed to distinguish safely

A blocked area should remain explicitly blocked.

It must not be smuggled downstream as “probably fine to build.”

---

### 13. Explicit "Do Not Hand This Off Yet" Rule

Do not hand an area to Engineering or coding AI as implementation-driving when the relevant source knowledge is still mostly:

- architecture direction
- strategic ambition
- issue inventory
- current-state observation without target-state clarification
- support-selection tension
- policy conflict
- scoring ambiguity
- unresolved evaluative logic
- rationale without contract clarity
- provisional capability grouping
- broad future-state narrative

In those cases, preserve the strongest justified non-contract surface and mark the area as non-handoff, blocked, deferred, or context-only as appropriate.

This is not slowness.

It is structural honesty.

---

### 14. Rules for Preserving Continuity During Rebaselining

Rebaselining must preserve continuity across:

- use cases
- flows
- meaningful steps and actions
- event-boundary-relevant distinctions
- support-selection surfaces
- unresolved meaning
- rationale and decision continuity

That continuity must remain visible even when the narrowed handoff package is smaller than the broader first-pass PKB.

Do not perform rebaselining by deleting or hiding the broader continuity chain.

Do not force the handoff package to carry all of that continuity as build authority.

Preserve it separately and visibly.

---

### 15. Rules for AI-Assisted Handoff

When preparing a rebaselined handoff for coding AI:

- give the AI the bounded implementation-driving subset as the primary contract surface
- give surrounding continuity pages only as explicitly labeled context
- preserve blockers and unresolved meaning explicitly
- do not imply that all provided files are equally authoritative for implementation
- do not let the AI infer missing behavior from preserved continuity, current-state capture, or approximations
- do not hand broad strategic target architecture to the AI as though it were the immediate build contract

If context is necessary, label it as context.

If contract is necessary, label it as contract.

If something remains blocked, label it as blocked.

---

### 16. Benchmark Validation Checks Against MKBE-Quality Output

A rebaselined handoff reaches benchmark quality only if all of the following are true:

- the narrowed package is visibly bounded and does not rely on tribal knowledge
- the implementation-driving subset is materially distinguishable from broader continuity/context surfaces
- capability anchors are clear enough that downstream work does not default to implementation-shaped structure
- behavioral contract surfaces are present where needed and absent where not yet justified
- support-selection, unresolved meaning, and rationale remain preserved without masquerading as build authority
- current-state and target-state remain visibly distinct where both matter
- the package is strong enough that Engineering or coding AI does not need to invent missing behavior to proceed
- the package is traceable back to the broader first-pass PKB without flattening the two into one undifferentiated corpus

A package that is merely smaller, cleaner, or easier to hand off is not benchmark-quality merely because it is tidier.

Benchmark quality requires safer narrowing, not prettier compression.

---

### 17. Failure Modes

The following are invalid rebaselining moves:

- treating every page in a valid first-pass PKB as equally implementation-driving
- deleting preserved continuity because it is not part of the first build slice
- leaving unresolved evaluative logic implicit and expecting Engineering or AI to “fill it in”
- using work items, prompts, or chat instructions as the hidden place where missing authority survives
- converting architecture ambition into pseudo-contract scope without explicit narrowing
- treating current-state capture as though it were already target-state authority
- forcing all important areas into draft specifications merely to make the handoff corpus look mature
- treating the narrowed handoff package as though it replaces the broader first-pass PKB rather than deriving from it

---

### 18. Outcome Standard

A successful rebaselined handoff is not one that looks small, elegant, or decisive.

A successful rebaselined handoff is one that:

- preserves continuity with the broader first-pass PKB
- identifies the actual implementation-driving subset honestly
- keeps context, continuity, and contract visibly distinct
- prevents local invention of unresolved behavior
- preserves deferred, excluded, blocked, and approximate knowledge explicitly where those materially affect interpretation
- gives Engineering and coding AI a safe bounded surface from which implementation can proceed without structural dishonesty

That is the standard this note is intended to support.
